# 幻棱 Phantaprism — 作品陈述

## 作品陈述（中文 · 建议展签/投稿用）

《幻棱》（Phantaprism）是一件互动生成艺术装置。观众在镜头前驻足的片刻，面孔被实时捕捉并送入一套由多个 AI 智能体协作的流水线：它们以近乎「侧写」的方式拆解微表情、气质与当下情绪，再经多轮讨论，最终输出一件抽象的、基于代码实时渲染的视觉作品。

我刻意不让它成为肖像。面孔在此不是被「画下来」的对象，而是被折射的入口——像白光穿过棱镜，在算法的层层弯折、分解与重组之后，落在屏幕上的，是一团与你有关、却不再属于你的光谱。它是讨论的结论，而不是你的镜像。

这件作品并不打算证明人工智能「理解」人类。恰恰相反，我想让观众直面的是算法凝视本身的质地：那种自信、系统、却往往彻底的误读。模型为你写下的每一个词、算出的每一项指标，都像一面哈哈镜——它照见的未必是你，而是机器如何分类、如何想象、如何命名这个世界。当你站在这个房间里，角色已经发生置换：人成为被读取的数据，而机器，成了那一端沉默却执着的观众。

技术路径上，作品以浏览器端的视觉追踪采集身体与面部信号，经由大语言模型驱动的多智能体编排生成概念与代码，最终以纯程序化的三维抽象场景呈现。每一次观看都可能触发不同的迭代，使「生成」本身成为展览现场不可重复的一部分。

## Artist Statement (English · optional)

*Phantaprism* is an interactive generative installation. When a viewer pauses before the camera, their face is captured in real time and fed into a pipeline of collaborating AI agents. The system performs something close to a forensic “profiling” of micro-expression, temperament, and mood, then holds a multi-round discussion among agents, and finally outputs an abstract, code-driven, real-time visual work.

It is not meant to be a portrait. The face is not an object to be depicted but an entry point for refraction—like white light through a prism. After successive algorithmic bends, decompositions, and reassemblies, what appears on screen is a spectrum related to you yet no longer yours. It is the conclusion of a debate, not your mirror.

The work does not argue that AI “understands” humanity. Rather, it exposes the texture of the algorithmic gaze: confident, systematic, and often thoroughly mistaken. Every label and metric is a funhouse mirror reflecting less who you are than how machines sort, imagine, and name the world. In this room, the roles reverse: the human becomes data, and the machine becomes the watching audience.

Technically, the piece uses in-browser vision tracking, a multi-agent orchestration layer powered by large language models, and purely procedural real-time 3D abstraction. Each encounter may trigger a different iteration, making “generation” an unrepeatable part of the exhibition moment.

**Keywords**: installation; generative art; algorithmic gaze; multi-agent systems; real-time rendering
